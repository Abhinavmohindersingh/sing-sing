Products & Services — Design Decisions
One Page, Full Range
Everything in one placeCranes, hoists, batteries, software, services — a buyer shouldn't have to hunt across five separate pages to understand what TES sells
Left sidebar = filter, not navigationClicking Cranes or Hoists doesn't take you away — it filters the gallery in place. The buyer stays oriented, nothing reloads
Nav bar mirrors the sidebarCranes, Hoists, Batteries, Services in the top nav go straight to the relevant filter — so a buyer arriving from any page lands exactly where they need to be
Gallery Grid — Why Not a List
3D renders need spaceTES will have 3D renders of every crane — a vertical list buries them; a grid gives each model a visual moment
2-column layoutWide enough for the image to read clearly, compact enough to show 4–6 models without scrolling — buyer gets orientation fast
Configure button per cardBuyer doesn't need to click into a model page just to start a build — the CTA is right there on the card
Spec Pills — Not a Table
Capacity · Jib length · TypeThe three things a crane buyer looks for first — on the card, no clicking required. This is the difference between a buyer staying and a buyer bouncing
Tables belong on the model pageFull spec sheets are for the decision stage — this page is for orientation. Keep it scannable
SEO — Every Card is a Landing Page
Each card links to its own URL/cranes/bq-5013 — "BQ 5013 tower crane Australia" becomes an indexable page Google can rank independently
Products & Services page itselfTargets "tower cranes for sale Australia", "construction hoists QLD", "battery energy storage construction" — broad top-of-funnel terms
Filter views create contextA buyer filtering to BQ signals intent — that click behaviour feeds remarketing and helps TES understand which product lines drive the most interest

Why One Page

Why This Page Exists
The build is the leadBy this point the buyer has invested 5–10 minutes configuring — they are qualified. This page captures that intent before it disappears
Four exits, one clear primaryDownload PDF is the primary CTA — it's tangible, shareable, and keeps TES in the buyer's inbox long after they leave the site
Submit inquiry sends the full specTES receives exactly what was configured — no "what model were you looking at?" back-and-forth. Converts a lead into a qualified RFQ instantly
Save & Return
B2B sales cycles are longA crane buyer rarely decides the same day — they take the spec to their board, their engineer, their site manager. Save & Return keeps them coming back
Profile creation as a side effectSaving a build requires an account — TES builds its own customer database without a CRM push or marketing spend
AI Companion — Post-Build
Flags mismatches before submissionIf the hoist rating is too low for the selected jib at full radius — the AI says so before TES has to. Reduces spec errors in RFQs
Upgrades suggested in contextNot a popup — a contextual recommendation after the build is done. Feels like advice, not upselling
One-click applyBuyer clicks "Apply SC300 upgrade" — the build updates instantly, PDF re-generates. No going back into the configurator
Shareable Build Link
One short URL, full spectes.com.au/b/TES-5013-xxx — the buyer pastes it into a WhatsApp message to their site engineer. No login, full build visible
TES sees every shareEvery link view is tracked — TES knows when a build is being circulated internally, which is a strong buying signal

Timeline — Why This Order
Website first — the configurator needs a home
The 3D configurator lives inside the TES website. Before we can build it, the design system, URL structure, product page templates, and hosting environment need to exist. Phase 1 is the foundation everything else runs on.
Configurator second — now it has context
By Week 7 the site is live and indexed. The configurator builds on top of the existing product pages and design language — consistent, not bolted on. Buyers who land on a product page can go straight into the configurator without a jarring transition.
AI companion third — it needs real data first
The AI companion is trained on actual configurator usage patterns — which models buyers build, where they drop off, what questions they ask. That data only exists once the configurator is live. You can't tune it before it's been used.
16 weeks is the total. Not per phase.
Phases overlap — configurator UX work begins in Week 5 while the site SEO and QA is running. The total is 16 weeks of parallel progress, not three sequential chunks added together.
